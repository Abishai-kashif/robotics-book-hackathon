# Module 1: Introduction to Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics

## Overview
Module 1 provides the foundational concepts for understanding Physical AI and its application in humanoid robotics. This module establishes the connection between digital AI systems and physical robot control, emphasizing embodied intelligence principles.

## Learning Outcomes
By the end of this module, students will be able to:
- Explain the principles of Physical AI and embodied intelligence
- Understand ROS 2 integration in physical systems
- Implement simulation-to-reality concepts in robotics
- Apply humanoid-centric design principles

## Prerequisites
- Basic understanding of AI and robotics concepts
- Familiarity with programming concepts
- Understanding of mathematical foundations (linear algebra, calculus)

## Module Structure

### Chapter 1: Introduction to Physical AI
- Understanding embodied intelligence
- The connection between digital AI and physical systems
- Physical AI vs. traditional AI approaches
- Applications in humanoid robotics

### Chapter 2: ROS 2 Integration in Physical AI
- ROS 2 as middleware for physical systems
- Node communication in physical contexts
- Message types and services for robot control
- rclpy implementations for physical AI

### Chapter 3: Simulation-to-Reality in Robotics
- Simulation environments for Physical AI
- Gazebo and Unity for robot simulation
- Bridging simulation and real-world implementation
- Pathways from simulation to physical deployment

### Chapter 4: Humanoid Robotics Fundamentals
- Humanoid form factors and design principles
- Bipedal locomotion and balance control
- Human-centered environments and interaction
- Humanoid-specific challenges in Physical AI

## Dependencies
This module has no external dependencies and serves as the foundation for subsequent modules in the textbook.

## Estimated Duration
4-5 weeks of study (based on 6-8 hours per week)

## Assessment Approach
- Practical exercises implementing Physical AI concepts
- Simulation-based projects demonstrating ROS 2 integration
- Design challenges applying humanoid-centric principles
