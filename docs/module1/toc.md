# Module 1: Table of Contents and Learning Objectives

## Module Overview
Module 1: Introduction to Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics provides foundational concepts for understanding Physical AI and its application in humanoid robotics. This module establishes the connection between digital AI systems and physical robot control, emphasizing embodied intelligence principles.

### Module Learning Outcomes
By the end of this module, students will be able to:
- Explain the principles of Physical AI and embodied intelligence
- Understand ROS 2 integration in physical systems
- Implement simulation-to-reality concepts in robotics
- Apply humanoid-centric design principles

## Constitutional Principles Integration

### Module Alignment with Physical AI Constitution
All chapters in Module 1 adhere to the 8 core constitutional principles:

1. **Physical AI First**: All content connects AI systems to the physical world
2. **ROS 2 Integration**: Robotic concepts use ROS 2 as middleware
3. **Sim2Real**: Includes simulation examples before real-world concepts
4. **Multi-Platform**: Supports NVIDIA Isaac, Gazebo, Unity platforms
5. **Vision-Language-Action**: Integrates LLMs, computer vision, robotic action
6. **Humanoid-Centric**: Prioritizes humanoid form factors and environments
7. **Conversational AI**: Includes GPT models or conversational components
8. **Hardware-Aware**: Acknowledges computational demands

Each chapter includes explicit connections to these principles and demonstrates their practical application in humanoid robotics contexts.
